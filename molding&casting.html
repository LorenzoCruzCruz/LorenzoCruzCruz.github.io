Molding and Casting

First off, pick your object! i decided to go with the Belen Coin, where it has one side with the B symbol, and the other of the crest. This way, I could use it for both the open-back nold and the 2-part mold.

To start with the open back mold, I made a box bigger than the coin--I'd rather it be too large than too little--and I glued the coin onto the base on the box. Mind you, there can't be any leakage whatsoever.

I decided that I wanted the B side to print, so I glued the sheild side of the coin onto the bottom with the B facing upwards.

Mix solution A with solution B until its a nice blueish color--make sure its half and half--then once its well mixed, be careful pourin. Make sure to cover every but, since the detail will be captured.

After waiting for a bit, tear apart the box, and once you have this solid, pour any colored other mold into it to give you these!

We didn't finish the 2-part mold, but to get to where i got, get a slab of clay and surround the coin on the B side, what way the crest/sheild is what sticks outwards.

Pour the solution onto the clay and the coin, and wait a bit.
