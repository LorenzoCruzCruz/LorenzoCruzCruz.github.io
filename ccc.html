Computer Controlled Cutting

For the design of the cut, I decided to use Affinity Designer 2 with this specific page layout.

Click the 3 bars on the upper left corner, and select the "Place" dropdown.

From the "Place" button, it'll take you to this page, where you'll pick "Photos"

My design was something I had drawn previously on a seperate app called Notability, So I took a screenshot and imported the same image here to trace.

Shrink and center the image.

This here is important: outline the white borders with the LINE tool, and trace over them. This'll create the outline that will be cut into the wood.

Repeat the same action for the circle ecompasing the design.

Click the behind function.

Delete the image set to be left with the outline of the design, this is what'll be designed and engraved on the wood design.

After sending it to the machine, you'll be left with this: these are the pieces that will be assembled.

This is what it should look like assembled!

Reuse this sketch since this sketch will also be the first step for the sticker portion of this project.

Select a section and color it! I made it the same color as the original image since i had color to it.

I picked this design set, you're free to pick your own!

I decided to use some more color since we had the variety of greens to pick from.

These were the color layers I picked to make the sticker in the first place.

After inserting them into the machine, you wind up with this design after stacking them on each other! The details were too finite, so it messed up the design a bit, but it should look like this!
